Study — 4h PO Rollover × OpEx × Extended

The pin holds.
Then it releases.

When a 4-hour Phase Oscillator rolls over from the distribution zone during the OpEx Friday → post-OpEx 1-5d window, and price is already extended on the weekly or monthly ATR, dealer gamma unwinds in the trading days that follow. 25 years of SPY: 62% of these signals produce a ≥1% intraday drop within 10 trading days, 46% produce a ≥1.5% drop, 38% a ≥2% drop. Median drawdown −1.16%. The tail prints big: May 2001 (−4.83%), Oct 2011 (−5.11%).

Methodology
Signal (V2 rollover)
  • Peak threshold: 4h PO reaches ≥ 80 (distribution zone entered)
  • Trigger: 4h PO crosses below 80 on the next bar (leaving distribution)
  • Sample: 81 signals over Jan 2000 → Apr 2026 on SPY (post-audit methodology)
OpEx Window
  • OpEx Friday: 3rd Friday of each month (monthly options expiration)
  • Qualifying window: signal fires on OpEx Friday itself or within the next 1–5 trading days
Extended Filter
  • Weekly ATR position: (signal_close − prev_week_close) / weekly_ATR_14 ≥ 0.618
  • OR Monthly ATR position: (signal_close − prev_month_close) / monthly_ATR_14 ≥ 0.618
  • Deep extension: same math but threshold ≥ 1.0 (past full weekly/monthly ATR)
Forward Measurement
  • Max drawdown from signal close: for each horizon, we find the lowest low across all bars in the window (may occur on any day within the window, not just day N). Drop % = (min_low − signal_close) / signal_close. This is a cumulative running drawdown — if day 7 prints the low, the 10d drawdown captures that day-7 move even though the window started on day 0.
  • Hit threshold = drawdown reaches target: for a ≥1% hit, we ask "did the running min-low reach −1% from signal close at any point in the window?" Close-to-close is not required.
  • Horizons: 1, 3, 5, 10 trading days post-signal (rolling max-drawdown up through end of day N)

The edge is tail expansion, not hit rate. The 5-day window under extension only hits 1% 46% of the time — roughly baseline. What changes materially is the 10-day window: hit rates climb sharply and the 25th-percentile drawdown extends further. The pin suppresses action around OpEx; dealer unwinds play out over the following 1-2 weeks.

Practical Takeaways

The 4h rollover is a watch signal, not an immediate trigger. At 1 day, only 31% of extended events hit 1%. The dealer pin suppresses breakouts in both directions while the monthly chain is alive. Sizing into weeklies expiring within 3 days of the signal is fighting the same flows that caused the pin.

Plan for 10-day expiries minimum. The 25th-percentile 10d drawdown is −2.34% and the worst printed −5.11% (Oct 2011). Long-dated puts — not weeklies — capture the release.

OpEx Friday + Post-OpEx Monday are the clean triggers. Those two buckets hit 67% and 75% at just 5 days (unfiltered). If the 4h rollover fires on the OpEx close or the following Monday, the setup is at its cleanest. Post-OpEx Tue/Wed/Thu weaken rapidly — by then, the pin is already gone and the information is stale.

Deep extension ≠ better edge at 5 days. Signals at weekly or monthly ATR ≥1.0 hit 1% at 5 days at 50% vs moderate extension (46%) — roughly matched. Strong momentum can resist a fast break. At 10 days, deep extension converges with moderate. Size for the tail, not the median.

Nulls concentrate in 2019–2021. Several "no drop" events came from the 2019–2021 stimulus era. If regime is unusually pinned (zero-rates, dealer long gamma), discount the signal. Hit years in the current sample (2000, 2001, 2007, 2011, 2017, 2019) were tighter liquidity environments where dealer unwinds actually produced vacuum effects.
