02 — The Bilbo Filter
Check the 1-hour PO before every GG entry
If 1h PO confirms direction, take the trade. Bull GG jumps from 63% to 78%. Bear GG jumps from 65% to 90%. Counter-trend PO? Skip it — completion drops to 51–54%.

Consider letting bearish Bilbo winners run. 66% chance of reaching the full ATR. That's higher than the baseline chance of completing the GG itself. Trail your stop.
03 — Best Entry
The simplest entry — 38.2% — is hard to beat.
Immediate entry wins. 63–65% completion, +10% ATR expected value, appears 100% of the time. The EMA 8 pullback is marginally better (+11% EV) and appears 97%. Everything deeper is worse.

04 — Your Stop
The trigger (23.6%) is the line. Period.
If the trigger holds: GG completes 84–89%. If it breaks: 45–51%. That's a 39-point swing. No other level comes close to this signal strength.

05 — The 3-Minute Close
Open in the box, confirm with a 3m close above the trigger.
Setup: SPY opens inside the trigger box (±23.6%), then a 3-minute bar closes above the call trigger. Overall 73.8% chance of hitting 38.2%. But clean runs — no close back below — hit 97.1%.

A 3m close back below the trigger is a kill signal. Clean morning triggers (before 14:00) are effectively 100%. If price closes back below, the trade is broken.
  • Median time to target: 18 min (6 bars)
  • First hour triggers: 81% overall hit rate
  • After 15:00: 29% — skip it
06 — When to Trade
Open triggers are the A+ setup. Afternoon is a coin flip.
If the GG fires at the open (gap through 38.2%), take it immediately. 86–88% completion, most within the first hour. Morning triggers (09:00) are still solid at 63–64%. After noon, you're looking at 50/50 or worse. Bullish at 15:00 rarely completes (16%).
