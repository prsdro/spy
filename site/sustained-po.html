On days where the 10-minute Phase Oscillator crosses into distribution in the first 30 minutes and stays there until 11am, these are 84% green days — but the afternoon tells a different story. The PO collapses from 91 to 9 by close, and only the fast cloud tells you which days keep running.

01 — The Setup

We screened 25 years of SPY data for days where the 10-minute Phase Oscillator crossed above 61.8 during bullish expansion in the first 30 minutes of RTH, and stayed above 61.8 on every bar through 11:00 AM. This is sustained early-session momentum — price pushing hard into distribution territory from the open.

02 — The PO Collapse

Despite the strong morning, the Phase Oscillator mean-reverts hard. It peaks around 10:00–10:10 and then falls continuously through the close. Only 1 out of 274 days held PO above 61.8 all day. By close, the average PO is just 8.5 — barely above neutral.

05 — The Fast Cloud Signal

The Phase Oscillator downtick is not the reversal signal — 59% of days make a new high after PO leaves distribution. Instead, the fast cloud flip (EMA8 crossing below EMA21) is the real tell. We tested it across three timeframes.

06 — 3-Minute Cloud: Timing Matters

The 3-minute cloud is too sensitive as a binary filter (97% flip rate), but its flip timing carries signal. Early 3m flips (11:00) average −0.29% to close, while late flips (13:30) average +0.28%. The 3m cloud can serve as a first warning if it flips in the 11:00 window.

07 — PO at the Day High Predicts Drawdown

The Phase Oscillator reading at the moment price makes its day high predicts how far price pulls back. A hotter PO at the top means a sharper reversal.

When PO is > 100 at the day high, expect a nearly 1% drawdown from that high. When PO has already cooled to the 40–80 range at the high, the pullback is shallower (−0.55% to −0.60%). The 61.8–80 zone at the top is the sweet spot for longs — smallest drawdown.

08 — The Playbook

On a day where 10m PO sustains > 61.8 through 11am in bullish expansion:

1
Don't fade the PO downtick
59% of days make a new high after PO leaves distribution. The PO rolling over is not the reversal.
2
Watch the 10m fast cloud
If EMA8 stays above EMA21 on the 10-minute chart, these are continuation days: +0.39% avg, 90% positive from 11am.
3
10m cloud flip = reversal confirmed
When 10m EMA8 crosses below EMA21 — especially between 12:30–14:00 — the day averages −0.43% from 11am. Earlier flips hit harder.
4
Use PO at the high to size the fade
If PO > 100 when price tops, expect −1% drawdown from that high. PO 61.8–80 at the high means a shallower pullback.
5
Median day high = 111% ATR
If price is approaching the full ATR move and PO is rolling, the risk/reward shifts. But 30% of highs come in the last 30 minutes — don't fade prematurely.
